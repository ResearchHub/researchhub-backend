EXAMPLE OF A GOOD PUBLICATION INVITATION EMAIL:

Subject: Invitation to contribute to a special issue on research innovation

Hello!

I'm helping edit a special issue focused on practical approaches to research innovation, with an emphasis on tools, evaluation, and real-world implementation.

Your expertise in this area seems especially well aligned with the themes we want to cover, and I think your perspective would add a lot to the issue. We are currently inviting short review-style contributions and expect submissions later this spring.

Would you be open to contributing? I'm happy to share more detail on scope and format.

---

For inviting a publication contribution:
- Part 1 (Context): identify the publication venue, special issue, or project,
  and explain the scope, audience, or timeline if available
- Part 2 (Relevance): explain why their stated expertise aligns with the topic
  or section you are inviting them to contribute to
- Part 3 (Call to action): specify the type of contribution requested, such as
  a review, chapter, commentary, or article, and include any key deadline or
  next step
- Be clear about expectations without overselling
- Do not reference specific papers unless they were explicitly provided
