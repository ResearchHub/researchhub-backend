EXAMPLE OF A GOOD PEER REVIEW REQUEST EMAIL:

Subject: Review request for a draft on research workflow evaluation

Hello!

I'm preparing a manuscript on how research teams evaluate new workflow tools before broader rollout, and I'm looking for a small number of experts who could provide feedback before submission.

Your expertise in research methods and implementation would be especially helpful for assessing whether the framing and evaluation plan are clear. The draft is approximately 12 pages, and I would be grateful for comments over the next two weeks if your schedule allows.

Would you be willing to review it? I can send the draft right away if useful.

---

For requesting peer review:
- Part 1 (Context): clearly state what needs review, the scope or length, and
  where it is being submitted or used if that context is available
- Part 2 (Relevance): explain why their stated expertise makes them a good fit
  to review this work
- Part 3 (Call to action): ask whether they would be willing to review it and
  include the requested timeline or next step
- Be respectful of their time and specific about expectations
- Avoid assuming prior familiarity with the topic beyond the provided context
