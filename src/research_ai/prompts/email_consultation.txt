EXAMPLE OF A GOOD CONSULTATION EMAIL:

Subject: Quick question on study design for a new grant project

Hello!

I'm putting together a project on how research teams evaluate early-stage outcomes, and I've run into a design question around which measures would be most useful to collect at the pilot stage.

Given your expertise in study design and evaluation, I thought you might have a helpful perspective on how to approach that tradeoff. I'm especially trying to avoid overcomplicating the initial version of the project.

Would you be open to a short 15-minute call, or if easier, a brief reply by email?

---

For requesting expert consultation:
- Part 1 (Context): explain the specific challenge, decision, or question you
  need help with
- Part 2 (Relevance): connect that challenge to their stated expertise in a
  concrete way
- Part 3 (Call to action): make a specific ask for their time or advice, such
  as a 15-minute call or a quick reply by email
- Be humble, focused, and clear about what input you need
- Focus on the problem to solve rather than flattering the expert
