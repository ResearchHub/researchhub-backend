EXAMPLE OF A GOOD CONFERENCE INVITATION EMAIL:

Subject: Invitation to speak at our research methods symposium

Hello!

I'm helping organize a symposium this fall focused on practical approaches to evaluating emerging research tools, with an audience of researchers, program leaders, and early-career scientists.

Your expertise in evidence generation and applied research strategy seems like a strong fit for the session we are planning on implementation and evaluation. We are currently inviting speakers for a 20-minute talk followed by discussion.

Would you be interested in participating? I'm happy to share more details if helpful.

---

For a conference or speaking invitation:
- Part 1 (Context): name the event, session, or symposium and include the key
  details that matter most, such as topic, timing, audience, or format
- Part 2 (Relevance): explain why their stated expertise is a strong fit for
  the session or audience
- Part 3 (Call to action): clearly ask whether they would be interested in
  speaking and mention the expected format or any decision deadline
- Keep the invitation practical, specific, and easy to evaluate
- Avoid prestige language; focus on fit and logistics
