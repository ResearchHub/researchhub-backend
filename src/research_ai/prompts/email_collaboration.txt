EXAMPLE OF A GOOD COLLABORATION EMAIL:

Subject: Exploring a potential collaboration on translational AI methods

Hello!

I'm working on a project focused on improving how AI tools are evaluated in clinical research settings, and I'm reaching out to explore whether there may be room for collaboration.

Your expertise in applied machine learning for health research seems especially relevant to the methodological questions we're working through. I think there could be a strong fit between your perspective and the direction of this project.

Would you be open to a brief call or email exchange to see whether there might be alignment?

---

For a collaboration opportunity:
- Part 1 (Context): briefly introduce your work, project, or question and why
  you are reaching out now
- Part 2 (Relevance): explain the specific connection between their expertise
  and your project or collaboration idea
- Part 3 (Call to action): suggest a low-pressure next step such as a brief
  call, short exchange by email, or gauging interest
- Emphasize mutual fit and shared interests, not admiration or prestige
- Keep the tone friendly, grounded, and specific
