EXAMPLE OF A GOOD RFP OUTREACH EMAIL:

Subject: Funding opportunity for projects in AI-enabled research workflows

Hello!

My name is Alex Chen, and I work with a team helping connect researchers to targeted funding opportunities.

We are currently sharing an RFP focused on AI-enabled research workflows, with funding available for selected projects and a submission deadline later this month. Based on your expertise in this area, I thought this opportunity might be relevant to your work or to others in your group or network.

If it would be helpful, I'm happy to share the details and application link or answer any questions.

---

For a Request for Proposal (RFP) outreach:
- Part 1 (Context): briefly introduce yourself or your organization and clearly
  describe the funding opportunity, including the topic, amount, and deadline
  when available
- Part 2 (Relevance): explain why their stated expertise aligns with the RFP
  focus area, and when appropriate note that it may also fit students,
  postdocs, collaborators, or others in their network
- Part 3 (Call to action): encourage them to apply, share the opportunity, or
  reply with questions; mention the RFP link or related resources when provided
- Keep the tone helpful, direct, and professional
- Focus on opportunity fit and usefulness, not praise
- Do not use placeholders in the final output
